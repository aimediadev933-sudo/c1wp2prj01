Project Overview

Mission

To build foundational technical skills in routing and switching for IT engineers from Afghanistan's public universities involved in the AfgREN network, conducted as part of WP2 under Asi@Connect with technical and financial support from TEIN*CC.

Scope

Intensive theoretical and hands-on training designed to improve participants' ability to manage and troubleshoot campus networks connected to AfgREN, supporting the long-term goal of building a robust NREN in Afghanistan.

Location & Beneficiary

Country: Afghanistan
Beneficiary: Public university IT staff

Project Objectives

Primary Objective

To improve the technical capacity of network engineers managing AfgREN in Afghanistan's public universities through foundational knowledge in routing and switching.

Sub-Objectives

  • Train engineers in basic routing and switching theory and practices
  • Enable participants to configure and troubleshoot routers and switches
  • Introduce basic Cisco IOS commands and tools
  • Promote the use of modern networking software and diagnostics
  • Facilitate knowledge exchange and cooperation among university IT staff

Workshop Activities

Opening & Coordination

Formal inauguration by MoHE IT Director, emphasizing workshop relevance and stakeholder cooperation.

4-Day Workshop Delivery

Structured lessons, lab-based learning, and group work focused on routing and switching using Cisco devices and simulators.

Guest Presentations

AfgREN delegates explained the role of NRENs globally and AfgREN's current infrastructure and future plans.

Assessment & Certification

Written evaluation on Day 4 with 90% achieving satisfactory scores, followed by certificate presentation.

Feedback Collection

Structured questionnaire completed by all participants to assess satisfaction and identify future training needs.

Networking Events

Group meals and social dinner to enhance cooperation and community-building among participants.

Key Outcomes

✓ Engineers Trained

20 Network Engineers from public universities received comprehensive training, enabling them to support their institution's network needs more effectively.

✓ Skills Enhancement

Participants learned essential router and switch configuration and troubleshooting skills using modern networking tools.

✓ Certification Success

90% of participants passed the written test and were certified by the Ministry of Higher Education.

✓ High Satisfaction

Strong positive feedback recorded, with most participants expressing interest in more advanced training opportunities.

Challenges & Future Directions

Identified Challenges

  • Limited 4-day duration insufficient for comprehensive coverage
  • Equipment constraints limiting individualized practice
  • Need for follow-up training to build on foundations

Recommended Solutions

  • Intermediate and advanced workshop series
  • Resource expansion with more tools and hardware
  • Wider participation from additional institutions

Project Overview

The Capacity Development Workshop for AfgREN Network Engineers was a comprehensive 4-day intensive training program designed to build foundational technical skills in routing and switching. Conducted under WP2 of the Asi@Connect initiative with support from TEIN*CC and the European Union, this workshop aimed to strengthen Afghanistan's National Research and Education Network (NREN) infrastructure.

The project focused on training IT engineers from Afghanistan's public universities, providing them with essential knowledge and hands-on experience in network management and troubleshooting. This initiative represents a crucial step toward building a robust and sustainable educational network infrastructure in Afghanistan.

Project Objectives

Primary Objective

To improve the technical capacity of network engineers managing AfgREN in Afghanistan's public universities through foundational knowledge in routing and switching.

Sub-Objectives

  • Train engineers in basic routing and switching theory and practices
  • Enable participants to configure and troubleshoot routers and switches
  • Introduce basic Cisco IOS commands and tools
  • Promote the use of modern networking software and diagnostics
  • Facilitate knowledge exchange and cooperation among university IT staff

Workshop Activities

  • Opening Ceremony: Formal inauguration by MoHE IT Director emphasizing workshop relevance
  • 4-Day Intensive Training: Structured lessons with lab-based learning and group work
  • Guest Presentations: AfgREN delegates explained NREN roles and future infrastructure plans
  • Practical Assessments: Written evaluations measuring knowledge acquisition
  • Networking Events: Social activities to enhance cooperation and community building
  • Certification Ceremony: Recognition and certificates for successful participants
